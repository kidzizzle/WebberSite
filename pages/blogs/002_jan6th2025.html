January 6th, 2025


Okay I hate New Year's Resolutions but I think this time I might be onto something.


Every time I try to get my life "together" it's because I see all these productivity people online saying they have all the answers and these few things will get your life together. And then there are the productivity people online who say "screw what all of those productivity people online say" and then tell you the same thing as those productivity online but with a different filter.


While the content in their videos may have not taught me anything no matter how many of them I have seen over the last few years, they definitely have taught me something: the hardest part of doing anything is starting it.


Yes that's corny I get it and it's not a novel take either but idk it is true for me and if pisses me off so much. I have so many cool ideas I want to do and I never follow through with them because I always psych myself out before I even get started with them. This website is one of the only things in a long time that I actually decided on and have not entirely abandoned already. I'm always in a state of paralysis where I don't think I'm going to be able to complete something or complete it well. It's not even impostor syndrome either that's when you're already doing a good job and feel like you aren't. Half of the time I'm not doing a job at all and I feel like that might be significantly worse.


The most interesting part is that once I do start something I usually surprise myself with how capable I am of completing it. For example I made a script for this website in Ruby that would update all of the navigation pages whenever I added a post. It worked but it sucked and was ugly because it was my first Ruby program. I then decided I wanted to refactor it to make it better but this time instead of refactoring in Ruby (which would not take me long at all), I decided I wanted to rewrite the program in C. This took me longer but felt like I was doing more, especially because I was already somewhat familiar with the language.


It had some issues with cross platform inconsistencies, and there was a simple fix. In my head I knew it was simple! But it took me three days from the time that I realized the issue, to the time that I actually went to solve it. The solution took me 15 minutes or less once I got to working on it, which is great! Great except for the fact that I took 3 days and 15 minutes to code a 15 minute solution.


This is obviously just one situation and sounds like procrastination, but I am also a procrastinator and this is not the same feeling. When I procrastinate, I have a deadline and I KNOW for a fact I'm going to make it before the deadline. If I'm procrastinating it's because I'm confident in my abilities and want to free up time elsewhere and only work on that task when I need to. This is different it's like the opposite feeling but with the same consequence. I am not at all confident in my abilities even though I should be, and I have plenty of time to complete the task, but I push it off for seemingly no reason.


I'm sure there's some reason for all of this, but I don't really care what the reason is anymore because posting this blog is basically my solution to it. I can buy as many notebooks as I want, try to schedule my life as much as I want, and read all the improvement slop in the world, but I think posting this is probably going to be the biggest impact on my entire life in terms of getting things done, because it is taking care of the hardest part for me.


Anyways, side quests as a part of getting it all together:


  1. Read more books but only awesome ones.
  2. Watch more movies (this sounds like leisure but I get something out of this).
  3. Program at least 4-5 times a week.
  4. Update this website more.

I really hope this works and I think it will. If it doesn't and you know me, remind me of this post so I can get back to it please and thanks.
